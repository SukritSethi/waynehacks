The Sun
The Sun is the closest star to Earth and is the center of our solar system. It is a massive ball of gas, primarily made up of hydrogen (about 74%) and helium (about 24%). The Sun's diameter is approximately 109 times larger than Earth, and it is so large that more than one million Earths could fit inside it. The Sun generates energy through nuclear fusion, where hydrogen atoms combine to form helium, releasing energy in the process. This energy provides warmth and light for life on Earth. The Sun's surface temperature is around 5,500 degrees Celsius, and it has a magnetic field that creates sunspots, solar flares, and coronal mass ejections.
Mercury
Mercury is the closest planet to the Sun and is the smallest planet in the solar system. It has a cratered surface and no atmosphere, making it the hottest planet during the day and coldest planet at night.
Venus
Venus is the hottest planet in the solar system, with temperatures that can reach up to 864 degrees Fahrenheit. It has a thick atmosphere made up mainly of carbon dioxide and sulfuric acid, creating a runaway greenhouse effect.
Earth
Earth is the third planet from the Sun and is the only planet known to have life. It has a magnetic field that protects us from solar wind and a variety of ecosystems that support a wide range of life forms.
Mars
Mars is known as the "Red Planet" due to its iron-rich soil. It has a thin atmosphere and polar ice caps made up of carbon dioxide and water. It is home to the largest volcano in the solar system, Olympus Mons, and the deepest canyon, Valles Marineris.
Jupiter
Jupiter is the largest planet in the solar system and has the most moons, with at least 79. It has a giant red spot, which is a massive storm that has been raging for hundreds of years.
Saturn
Saturn is known for its rings, which are made up of ice particles and debris. It has at least 82 moons and is the second-largest planet in the solar system.
Uranus
Uranus is an ice giant, and it is unique in that it rotates on its side. It has a faint ring system and at least 27 moons.
Neptune
Neptune is the farthest planet from the Sun and is another ice giant. It has a dark spot, similar to Jupiter's red spot, and at least 14 moons. It is also the windiest planet in the solar system, with winds that can reach up to 1,200 miles per hour.
Pluto
Pluto used to be considered a planet but has since been reclassified as a dwarf planet. It has a heart-shaped glacier on its surface and at least five moons. It is the largest object in the Kuiper Belt, a region of icy bodies beyond Neptune.
